Workshop library

Sessions I've designed and run.

Each of these started the same way: a real behaviour I wanted to shift in the room, then a design that works backwards from it. None of them are lectures. Every one makes participants generate their own evidence before it asks them to change anything.

Every session opens the same way — on purpose

Before anything else, participants get a small piece of paper and write down whatever's on their mind right now — a worry, a stray thought, a thing they keep circling back to. It does three jobs at once: it clears mental noise so people are actually present, it lowers the cost of honesty for everything that follows, and it quietly introduces journaling — the reflection tool the rest of the work depends on. Only then do we choose, together, which topics the room most needs.
