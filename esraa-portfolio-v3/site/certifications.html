Certifications & recognition

What I've trained in — and what it changed.

A certificate is only worth the practice it changed. Here's what each one actually did to how I work.

TTT

Train-the-Trainer

Xceed

This is where facilitation stopped being instinct and became method. Train-the-Trainer gave me a structured way to think about how adults learn in a room — how to set up an activity, run a debrief, handle the quiet person and the dominant one, and recover when a session drifts. Almost everything on my approach page traces back to the discipline this built.

YP

Youthpass — recognition of learning outcomes

Erasmus+ framework

Working within the Youthpass framework on Erasmus+ programmes changed how I write objectives. Youthpass forces you to name competences in terms a learner can recognise and evidence — which is exactly the discipline of writing observable objectives. It's why I now refuse to write a goal I can't picture someone demonstrating.

BSc

BSc, Science and Education — Physics

Ain Shams University · 2017–2021

The formal foundation — and a genuinely useful lens. A physics-and-education degree trains you to model what's happening underneath the surface and to teach it to someone who doesn't yet see it. That's the whole job of a learning designer, just pointed at different content.
